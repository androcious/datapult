Inspiration

In his book, Everybody Lies: Big Data, New Data, and What the Internet Can Tell Us About Who We Really Are, Economist Seth Stephens-Davidowitz tells us that in the 2016 presidential election, a state's search history for a given candidate was a better predictor for a candidate's success than the traditional methods of polling. We think we can leverage this to make a unique prediction tool for the 2020 Democratic Primary.

Polls are historically unreliable in predicting the outcome of an election. In the 2016 Presidential Election, every major poll showed Hillary Clinton winning the election by a wide margin on the day of the election. As we all know, this isn't quite how things panned out. What if we could leverage the public's everyday search activity to predict an election?
